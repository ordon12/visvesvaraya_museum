About the Museum

The Visvesvaraya Science Museum, located in Bengaluru, is dedicated to the memory of Bharat Ratna Sir M. Visvesvaraya. It houses various scientific experiments and engines, and was inaugurated by the first Prime Minister of India, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, on 14 July 1962.

History

Foundation and Inauguration

The museum was established to honor Sir M. Visvesvaraya, a renowned engineer and statesman. It was inaugurated by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru on 14 July 1962.

Development Over the Years

Over the decades, the museum has expanded its exhibits and educational programs, becoming a major center for science education in India.

Significant Milestones

Some of the key milestones include the addition of the Space Technology Gallery and the Engine Hall, which have attracted numerous visitors.

Exhibits

Permanent Exhibits

The museum features several permanent exhibition halls, including:

  • Engine Hall: Showcasing various engines and mechanical devices.
  • How Things Work: Interactive exhibits explaining the principles of physics and engineering.
  • Space Technology Gallery: Exhibits related to space exploration and technology.

Temporary Exhibits

The museum also hosts temporary exhibits on various scientific topics, ensuring there is always something new to explore.
